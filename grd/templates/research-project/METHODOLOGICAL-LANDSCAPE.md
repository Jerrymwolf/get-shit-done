<guidelines>

This corresponds to the methods assessment in a systematic review protocol (PRISMA-P; Cochrane Handbook Ch. 3). Focus on HOW the field studies this topic, not WHAT it has found.

**Research Methods Inventory:**
- Catalog the full range of methods used in this domain
- Note which methods dominate and which are emerging
- Include both quantitative and qualitative traditions
- Identify gaps -- methods that could be used but haven't been

**Validated Instruments & Scales:**
- Prioritize instruments with published psychometric properties
- Note adaptation history (e.g., translated versions, shortened forms)
- Distinguish between validated, adapted, and emerging instruments
- Include interview protocols and qualitative tools, not just scales

**Research Design Patterns:**
- Show the link between research question type and design choice
- Include both experimental and observational designs
- Note sample size norms for each design in this field
- Identify designs that are under-used relative to questions asked

**Methodological Evolution:**
- Track paradigm shifts in how the field collects evidence
- Identify assumptions baked into historical methods
- Note where new methods have opened previously inaccessible questions

**Data Sources & Datasets:**
- Include both primary data collection norms and secondary datasets
- Note access restrictions that affect replicability
- Identify datasets that are frequently re-analyzed vs. underexplored

</guidelines>
